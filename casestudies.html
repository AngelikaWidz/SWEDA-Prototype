Business Support

Sri Lankan Café

The Sri Lankan Café, based in Sandwell, had become popular for authentic Sri Lankan food but faced limitations due to seating capacity and equipment needs. Through participation in the Community Led Business Support project, the business attended workshops and was referred to the Sandwell Business Growth Team.

When a larger space became available in the new West Bromwich Indoor Market, the business was able to move into a site with greater footfall and more potential customers. This represented a significant growth opportunity and demonstrated the value of practical support, workshops and referral pathways.

Self-employment around family life

One client came to SWEDA as a mother of two young children with limited childcare support. She wanted to explore self-employment as a route into work that could fit around her family responsibilities.

Claire supported her to develop a business plan for a hair extensions business. Because the client had dyslexia and found written planning difficult, the support was broken down step by step. Claire helped her understand what each section of the business plan required, liaised with the Jobcentre and connected her with Craig in the IT Suite to type the plan and access digital support.

The support helped the client access a training pathway and move closer to starting her own business.

“I am really grateful to SWEDA for their support and the opportunity I have had to make a better life for myself and my children.”

Digital Skills

From first-time computer user to writing a book

One learner came to SWEDA having never used a computer before. During a difficult employment tribunal, they needed to send important documents to a solicitor but had no idea how to do so.

Craig and the digital skills team provided patient support, first helping the learner upload documents and then gradually building their confidence using a computer. Over time, the learner bought their own laptop, began attending regularly, completed an Artificial Intelligence course and started writing their first book.

This case shows the life-changing effect of digital inclusion. What began as crisis support became a pathway into confidence, creativity and lifelong learning.

“I am currently writing my first book with the help of Craig. I know I have a lot to learn, but I am confident that with the help of SWEDA, I am actually writing this book instead of just dreaming about it.”

Digital Skills

Gary Poulton

Gary Poulton came to SWEDA struggling with digital tasks. He could not check his Universal Credit journal, book GP appointments online or support his daughter with homework. Health issues had kept him out of work, and digital exclusion made life feel even more overwhelming.

SWEDA helped Gary create a CV and referred him to Craig for digital skills support. Starting with the basics, Gary learned how to use a computer, browse the internet, check emails and build confidence online.

Gary now feels more independent and in control of his life. He can go online by himself, support his daughter and consider future part-time work

“SWEDA made me feel like I mattered. They didn’t rush me or make me feel stupid.”

Employment Support

Adapting after redundancy

Samantha Harrison came to SWEDA after being made redundant from a Warehouse Administrator role she had held for 21 years. The redundancy was a major shock, and she felt unsure about the modern job market, CVs and interviews.

SWEDA helped her build a professional CV from scratch, prepare for interviews and identify roles that matched both her skills and family responsibilities. Within two months, Samantha secured a new Warehouse Administrator role.

Her story shows the importance of tailored employment support for people who have strong work histories but need help adapting to a changed recruitment environment.

“I’m no longer afraid of change because I’ve learned how to adapt.”

Employment Support

Employment and confidence progression

Not every employment journey begins with a job outcome. Some residents first need to rebuild confidence, routines and self-belief. Through MADE and other programmes, SWEDA supported people who had been out of work for many years to recognise transferable skills, understand the labour market and begin applying for roles.

Ntrisha Allmark, for example, had been out of work for over 15 years while raising her family. Through CV, interview, transferable skills and job search workshops, she moved from feeling unsure and unmotivated to actively seeking part-time work and believing she had something to offer.

“I’ve gone from thinking, ‘I’m not ready’ to thinking, ‘I can do this.’”

Welfare Advisory Service

Severe Disability Premium backdated payment

One client came to SWEDA after receiving a migration letter requiring a move from income-related ESA to Universal Credit. The client had long-term health conditions and anxiety and needed support to avoid losing out financially.

Simon carried out a full review and identified that the client may have been missing Severe Disability Premium since 2021. With consent, Simon contacted the ESA helpline and initiated an application for backdated payments.

The client later received confirmation that Severe Disability Premium had been backdated to 2021, resulting in £13,636.70 being awarded.

This case demonstrates the importance of full benefit checks before major transitions to Universal Credit.

“Thank you Simon for all your support and hard work helping me.”

Silver Linings: Support for older residents

Silver Linings supported older residents to navigate benefit entitlements, pension transitions, Attendance Allowance and household financial changes.

Derek Harrison and his wife received support during her transition from Universal Credit to State Pension. Simon carried out benefit checks, helped complete Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction forms, checked Pension Credit entitlement, applied for help with NHS costs and referred the couple to LEAP for energy advice.

“Since the first time I went to SWEDA the staff and Simon have been amazing, polite and friendly.”
