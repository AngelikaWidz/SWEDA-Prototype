Supporting people to build brighter futures

For over 30 years, SWEDA has been helping people across Sandwell and the Black Country build skills, confidence and opportunities for the future. Through employment support, business advice, digital skills training, English language learning and welfare guidance, we empower individuals to overcome barriers and achieve their goals.

How Can We Help?

At SWEDA, we understand that taking the first step isn't always easy. That's why our services are designed to meet you where you are, providing friendly, personalised support that helps you build confidence and achieve your ambitions at your own pace.

Whether you need help finding work, learning new skills or accessing advice, we're here to support you. Explore our services below or contact our team to find out more.

  • Friendly and supportive advisors
  • Support tailored to your circumstances
  • No judgement, no pressure
  • Help at your own pace
  • Connected to local opportunities across the Black Country

Success Stories

Returning to work after unemployment

One client came to SWEDA after nearly six months out of work. They had lost their role as a Washroom Technician, had an outdated CV, and were struggling to move forward despite previous experience in customer service and retail.

SWEDA helped them create a new CV, attend Job Club and access an accredited customer service course through Sandwell College, which included a guaranteed interview with Tesco. The client received one-to-one support with their online application and interview preparation.

As a result, they secured a full-time role at Tesco in April 2025. Beyond the job outcome, the client reported renewed confidence, financial stability, and a sense of purpose.

Employment Support

Launching a small business from scratch

A local resident came to SWEDA with an idea for a home-baking business but no experience writing a business plan or managing finances.

Our business advisors worked with her over several sessions to develop a costed plan, register the business, and identify a small grant to buy equipment.

She now supplies three local cafés and is exploring taking on her first member of staff.
